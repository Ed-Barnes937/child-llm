# Competitive Landscape

## Key Players
- **Genie (Google)** — child-aimed AI chatbot, educational lean. Part of Google's family products ecosystem.
- **Moxie** — physical robot companion for ages 5-10. Hardware play, expensive, different form factor.
- **Khanmigo (Khan Academy)** — AI tutor on GPT-4. Socratic approach. School-focused, curriculum-coupled.
- **Bark, Qustodio, Google Family Link** — parental monitoring/filtering for existing apps. Don't provide an experience, just restrict.
- **YouTube Kids, Messenger Kids** — child-safe versions of adult products. Precedent for the model.

## Gaps in the Market
- No standalone, model-agnostic product with parental control as a first-class feature
- Existing products are either locked to an ecosystem or offer binary safe/not-safe with no granularity
- Nobody owns the "curiosity companion" space — Khanmigo is homework, Genie is Google-locked
- Parental customisation is an afterthought everywhere

## Our Angle
The differentiator isn't "child-safe AI" generically — Google can do that. It's **parent-controlled, curiosity-driven AI** where families set their own boundaries. The same instinct that makes parents uneasy about unsupervised Google search applies to unsupervised LLM access — and nobody is solving that with real parental agency.

## Moat Consideration
Tech alone won't be the moat — LLM APIs are commoditised. The moat is:
- Trust relationship with parents
- Quality of the guardrails/controls UX
- Getting the "curiosity companion" tone right so kids actually want to use it
